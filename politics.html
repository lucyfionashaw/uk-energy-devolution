Section 02 · Party

Does the town hall's party matter?

Each local application is decided by a council, and councils have a political colour. By joining every project to the party in control of its planning authority, we can ask a sharp question: does who runs the council change what gets built? The honest answer — only for the technology people can see from their window.

All technologies

Approval by who controls the council

Across all locally-decided projects, approval is high under every party — between 85% and 93%. At this altitude, partisanship barely registers. The interesting variation is hiding inside one technology.

The contested technology

Onshore wind is where party bites

Strip out the easy approvals and look only at onshore wind — the visible, contested, neighbour-bothering technology. Now the parties separate cleanly, in exactly the order you might expect.

Onshore wind: approval rate by controlling party

REPD Q1 2026, onshore wind only, decided applications.
Local control matters here

Conservative-controlled councils approve onshore wind at 61%, against Labour's 69%, the Lib Dems' 71% and the SNP's 75%. A 14-point spread on the one technology where a turbine on the ridgeline is a live local fight.

Contested vs uncontested

For everything else, party disappears

Put the three big technologies side by side. Solar and batteries are approved at 88%+ under every party — the spread collapses to a few points. Partisanship only surfaces where the build is visible and contested.

Aligned with Westminster?

Does it matter if the council matches the government?

A natural hypothesis: councils run by the same party as the UK government wave projects through. The data says — barely, overall. And where it does show up, the cause is tangled.

All technologies

Council party = Westminster party, vs differs.

Onshore wind only

The aligned bar is lower — see the caveat.

Read this descriptively, not causally

Across all technologies, alignment makes almost no difference (90.0% vs 88.8%). For onshore wind, "aligned" councils approve less (60.9% vs 67.1%) — but that is mostly an artefact: the aligned years are dominated by Conservative councils under a Conservative government during the 2015–2023 onshore-wind moratorium. This cut describes a correlation, not a cause.

A note on Northern Ireland

Northern Ireland's councils could not be matched to this Great Britain party-control dataset, so its ~590 projects sit outside the party analysis. The four-nations comparisons on the devolution page still include NI in full.
