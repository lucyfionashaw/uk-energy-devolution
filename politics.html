Does party matter?

Does the council's party change what gets built?

Every local project is decided by a council, and councils have a political colour. Crediting each decision to the party that controlled the council on the day it was made, here is who approves what — by capacity, per unit of land, and how fast.

Capacity & speed

Who approves the most, and how fast

The Conservatives have approved the most capacity outright — they run the most councils and the most land. Labour-run councils are the fastest of the major parties to reach a decision.

Per unit of land

Approval per km² of land controlled

Raw capacity favours whoever governs the most countryside. Normalising by the land each party actually controlled — MW approved per km², per year — reorders it: dense urban councils approve the most for the land they have. Labour is the densest of the major parties; the Greens are higher still, but on only a handful of councils.

Approval rate

The rate they say yes

Approval rates are high under every party — roughly 85% to 93% of decided projects. Labour-run councils approve at a slightly higher rate than Conservative ones, but at this altitude the gaps are small.

A note on Northern Ireland

NI councils sit outside these charts — they use STV power-sharing and a separate, shorter control series, so they aren't part of the GB party comparison.
